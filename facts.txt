Approximately 30,000 species per year — about three per hour — are being driven to extinction. – Harvard University (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
Approximately 80 percent of the decline in global biological diversity is caused by habitat destruction. – African Conservancy (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
An average of 96 elephants are killed for their ivory in Africa each day. – Wildlife Conservation Society (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
Approximately 60 percent of elephant deaths are at the hands of poachers. – Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
A century ago wild dog packs in Africa typically consisted of approximately 100 members. Now the average pack size is 10. – American Museum of Natural History (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
Every year poachers take more than 38 million animals from the wilds of Brazil to meet the global demand for illegal wildlife. Most are birds destined to become caged pets for people in Rio de Janeiro, Sydney, Madrid or New York. – National Geographic (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
Approximately 6,000 tigers are currently being kept as pets in the United States. The number is greater than the number of tigers in the wild throughout the world. – Born Free (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
Africa’s lion population has decreased in size by approximately 42 percent during the past two decades. – African Wildlife Foundation (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
Three rhinoceroses are killed by poachers on an average day. – One Green Planet (https://www.animalmatters.org/facts/wildlife/)
